The [Gemma 4 Good hackathon](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/gemma-4-good-hackathon/overview) hosted by Kaggle and Google DeepMind has concluded. It centered around using these relatively small and open-weights models “to drive positive change and global impact”. 14,523 entrants were recorded, and 1,613 submission were counted.

My submission is [Aileen 4 Disaster Relief](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/gemma-4-good-hackathon/writeups/new-writeup-1778759643211): “from field evidence to public support”.

The project is inspired by Cyclone Narelle in Western Australia and the wildlife-rescue work shared by [Brinkley Davies](https://www.brinkleydavies.com) and [Balu Blue Foundation](https://balubluefoundation.org). It focuses on one practical layer: how field evidence becomes public support when rescuers are overloaded.

[One update](https://www.instagram.com/p/DWqbCVOkjAK/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link&igsh=MzRlODBiNWFlZA==) made the strain concrete: over 150 calls or reports within the first 72 hours, a shed turned into an animal clinic - and by Day 8, still no power or water, no signal, no laptop, and no admin or help with social-media.
