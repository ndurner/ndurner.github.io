OpenAI released “[Codex app](https://developers.openai.com/codex/app)”, a nice GUI re-imagination of Codex CLI. It’s currently only available for macOS, with support for Linux and Windows coming “in a week or two”. While it, as the other Codex products, continues to be positioned as centered around coding, suitability for tasks other than coding was confirmed on the Codex AMA on X: possible tasks include testing, deploying, interacting with Figma. Or video editing: find interesting moments in a video.

The Codex App can be thought of ChatGPT with access to local files and other Agentic AI features like Skills or Automations. (The question on why there is a separation between ChatGPT and Codex App was posed on the AMA, but not answered due to technical difficulties; Skill are textual instructions on how to perform certain tasks).

Codex App introduce the new term “thread”. This is essentially the same as a conversation in ChatGPT or a session (created with "/new”, restored with “/history” or “codex resume”) in Codex CLI.
