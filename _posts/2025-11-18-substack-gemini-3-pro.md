The much anticipated Google release of [Gemini 3](https://deepmind.google/models/gemini/) has yielded Gemini 3 Pro ([blog post](https://blog.google/products/gemini/gemini-3/)) for [developers](https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/gemini-3?thinking=high) - the biggest of their Gemini variants, without the smaller Flash and Flash Lite variants. The [Model Card](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/Model-Cards/Gemini-3-Pro-Model-Card.pdf) is pretty light on details and is only 8 pages long. In short, it appears to be a powerful, very expensive, agentic reasoning language model.

Also released was Gemini 3 Deep Research (see [same blog post](https://blog.google/products/gemini/gemini-3/)).

It does note SOTA improvements across the(ir) board, except for SWE-Bench Verified, where Sonnet 4.5 has an edge. Gemini 3 Pro jumps to #1 on the aggregate [Artificial Analysis Intelligence leaderboard](https://artificialanalysis.ai/models/gemini-3-pro), dethroning GPT-5.1 High. It is the [second-most expensive model](https://artificialanalysis.ai/models/gemini-3-pro#cost-to-run-artificial-analysis-intelligence-index) however: only Grok 4 is more expensive to run on their benchmark.
